DBS FinTech Hackathon Pitch

CashFlow Shield

An internal DBS workbench for relationship managers and credit inspectors to detect SME cash-flow stress early, explain the drivers, and act through responsible lending guardrails.

1. Problem

SME cash stress is visible before default, but banks often respond too late.

Manual triage

RMs manually inspect transactions, invoices, fixed costs, and client notes to understand liquidity stress.

Late intervention

By the time an SME asks for help, payroll, suppliers, or receivables may already be under pressure.

Risk control

Fast support still needs affordability, evidence, suitability, and policy approval.

2. User

This is for DBS internal teams, not customer self-service.

RM

Uses the tool to identify watch-list clients, prepare outreach, and request evidence.

Credit inspector

Validates risk drivers, checks policy readiness, and blocks unsuitable submissions.

Portfolio lead

Monitors pilot quality, false positives, overrides, and adoption across RM teams.

3. Solution

CashFlow Shield turns client cash-flow data into a human-reviewed early-warning workflow.

  1. Import client cash-flow CSV or use internal operating-account signals.
  2. Stress expected receivables and forecast 30-day liquidity buffer.
  3. Decompose risk into explainable factors for RM and inspector review.
  4. Generate a draft action plan with policy gates and non-credit alternatives.

5. What We Built

A working frontend prototype, not just slides.

Frontend

HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Runs locally in the browser with no backend dependency.

Data

CSV upload/paste, local persistence, sample cash-flow file, and SingStat/MAS benchmark refresh fallback.

Workflow

Role settings, overview factors, cash forecast, deep risk drivers, action plan, evidence, and pilot plan.

6. Model Logic

The score is explainable because it is decomposed into seven review factors.

Bufferminimum forecast balance
Receivablesinvoice delay and concentration
Fixed costspayroll, rent, tax, suppliers
Readinessdata confidence and policy gate

The model is inspired by cash-flow underwriting and early-warning credit monitoring: deposits, balances, withdrawals, volatility, negative balance events, coverage, and evidence quality.

7. Innovation

The innovation is not auto-lending. It is explainable, proactive RM intervention.

  • Moves DBS from reactive SME support to forward-looking liquidity monitoring.
  • Combines cash-flow stress testing, explainable drivers, and responsible lending controls in one workflow.
  • Uses public benchmarks as context while preserving internal policy authority.

8. Risk Controls

Banking realism is built into the product.

No auto-approval

The system generates a draft RM plan only. Credit submission can be blocked.

Evidence-first

High-risk cases require invoice evidence, affordability review, and borrower exposure checks.

Auditability

A production version would log factor scores, source data version, overrides, and final RM action.

10. Why This Wins

CashFlow Shield is narrow enough to demo, realistic enough for a bank, and valuable enough to pilot.

  • Clear problem: late SME liquidity intervention and manual RM triage.
  • Clear demo: upload data, stress cash flow, explain drivers, generate guarded actions.
  • Clear DBS fit: internal RM workflow, credit-inspector controls, and measurable pilot path.
